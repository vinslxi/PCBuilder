What is a Computer?

A computer is an electronic object, which is aimed at keeping, retrieving, and processing information. The computer was first known as a term that was used to denote human beings who carried out complicated mathematical operations using a device like the abacus or slide rule. Gradually, these tasks started to be performed by mechanical devices and the name of the machine was called the computer. Today, computers are electronic in their processing of data, and they operate in the same way as the simple functions of input, processing, output, and storage, usually referred to as the IPOS cycle. A computer is an electronic machine that involves data processing and execution of tasks based on a collection of instructions known as a program. It receives input either as input of the user or any other source, processes it, and gives back the output in a convenient form.

Hardware is defined as the physical objects including the central processing unit (CPU), memory, storage devices and input/output peripherals. Software conversely contains programs and operating systems that give directions to the hardware. Computers can do many tasks such as simple calculation to the complex simulations and data analysis. They can process binary code a system of ones and zeros to process and handle information. The use of modern computers has become important in virtually all the spheres of our daily life education, communication, business, science and entertainment. Computers come in various categories, including desk computers, laptop computers, tablets, and server computers that have different applications. An example would be the use of supercomputers in scientific research where it would be imperative to have enormous processing power. In general, a computer is an essential device that will help increase productivity, better decision-making and will unite people all over the globe with the help of digital technology.

Why study Computer?

Where do computers come from?

The computer history can be traced back to the early inventions of mankind, which were made to make counting, calculation and record keeping easier. The abacus was one of the earliest known devices and it was invented thousands of years ago to perform simple arithmetic operations. Over time, there were more elaborate machines created to solve mathematical problems more effectively. Charles Babbage was an inventor and English mathematician who in the 19th century designed the Analytical Engine which is frequently considered as the first concept of a programmable mechanical computer. His invention brought about the concept of utilizing memory and processing units which are major characteristics of modern day computers. In the middle of the 20 th century, the first electronic computers were constructed by use of vacuum tubes. They were also gigantic and devoured a lot of electricity, but they were faster than the previous mechanical models. This was later overtaken by the revolution of the industry in the 1950s and 1960s through the invention of transistors and microchips, which made computers smaller, faster, and more reliable. Nowadays, computers are also designed by sophisticated individuals, and they can be assembled using components manufactured in different parts of the globe, especially silicon chips produced in places like China, Taiwan, and the U.S.
